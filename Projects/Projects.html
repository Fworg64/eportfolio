NASA Robotic Mining Competition

Scope

The NASA Robotic Mining Comptetion is a competion put on by NASA where university teams compete to build a fully autonomous mining robot for intergalactic operations. The official rules for the competition may be found here.

Involvement

I was responsible for the Obstacle Avoidance System. This involved 2 semesters of reasearch in computer vision and several device iterations. At first a structered light sensor was developed, but after sufficient optics could not be developed within budget constraints to perform in the lighting conditions that would be present, the solution shifted to an image processing algorithm. An academic writeup is currently in progress.

Soccer Robot Reasearch Project

Scope

This project consisted of proving the feasibility of a low cost localization and control system where a clear line of site is present.

Involvement

Our team consited of 2 undergraduate Electrical Engineers and one graduate Computer Science Major. Our reasearch constited of a set of small robots which coordinated to play robot soccer. An academic paper is available for download here.

DuckPond Mobile Game

Scope

DuckPond is a mobile game developed for the Google Admob Competition in 2016-2017. It is available for iOS and Android.

Involvement

DuckPond was developed by 2 people: Lois S. Dilone and Austin F. Oltmanns who did the graphics and programming respectivly. The game was written in Java and its promotional page may be seen here.
